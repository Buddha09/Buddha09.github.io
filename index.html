01 — About

A physicist studying
the shape of data.

I am a doctoral researcher in the Department of Physics at the National Institute of Technology Sikkim, working on applications of topological data analysis to nonlinear dynamics.

My work uses persistent homology and related tools to detect regime shifts, extreme events, and causal structure in financial markets and dynamical systems — turning abstract topology into concrete inference.

Research Interests
  • Topological Data Analysis
  • Nonlinear Dynamics & Complex Systems
  • Financial Time-Series Analysis
  • Persistent Homology & Euler Characteristic
